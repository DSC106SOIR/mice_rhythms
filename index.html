Mice Rhythms

Explore temperature rhythms in female and male mice using two interactive modes: Granularity and Time.

Understanding Mice Rhythms

This interactive visualization allows us to explore the daily temperature cycles of male and female mice. Using the Granularity Mode, you can investigate how temperature and activity patterns vary by minute, hour, or day, and filter for reproductive cycle phases in females.

In Time Mode, you can slide through each moment of the experiment to observe how individual mice react. The background shifts between night and day to mirror the circadian rhythm, helping us better contextualize the data. Notice that mice are nocturnal, so the lighter areas on the graph correspond with when mice are asleep. Area of each dot corresponds with the activity of the mouse in that moment.

This tool was designed to support research into how biological rhythms vary across sexes and reproductive states in controlled environments.
